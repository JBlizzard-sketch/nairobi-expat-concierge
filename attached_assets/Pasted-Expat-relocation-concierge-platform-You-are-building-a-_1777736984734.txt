Expat relocation concierge platform


You are building a full production-grade digital relocation concierge platform for professionals moving to Nairobi. Read the full brief, expand the vision, present ~20 substantial phases, wait for "next."
The pain:
Nairobi receives thousands of expatriates every year — UN and UNEP staff, World Bank consultants, NGO country directors, multinational regional managers, embassy personnel. Each one arrives needing the same things: a vetted house in the right neighbourhood, school shortlists for their children, a reliable car, internet sorted, domestic help found and vetted, a local bank account opened, and some orientation about how Nairobi actually works. This process currently takes weeks of chaotic Google searches, expat Facebook group posts, real estate agent chases, and stressful guesswork. The employer (UN, NGO, corporate) often has a relocation budget and a HR team that desperately wants a single vendor to handle this. No one has built this cleanly for Nairobi. The few relocation "agents" that exist are informal, unscalable, and have no digital presence.
What you are building:
A platform where an incoming expat (or their employer's HR team) fills in a relocation profile — arrival date, family size, budget, employer, school-age children, neighbourhood preferences, specific needs — and gets back a customised relocation plan with curated options in every category. The platform acts as the orchestration layer: it doesn't own the housing or the schools, but it has vetted relationships with the best landlords, school admissions offices, car hire companies, ISPs, and domestic staff agencies, and it coordinates everything through one account manager and one dashboard. Corporate HR teams pay a flat relocation package fee ($300–800 depending on complexity). Individual expats can self-serve at a lower tier.
Critical nuances:
The target customer is genuinely time-poor and money-flexible — they will pay for speed and reliability, not for the cheapest option. Every recommendation must be genuinely vetted — a bad housing recommendation destroys the brand immediately. The school placement piece is complex — Braeburn, ISN, Brookhouse, Banda all have different admissions processes and waitlists, and the platform must have real intelligence on this (see idea #7 in the original list). The corporate HR sale is the most valuable channel — one HR manager at a UN agency can send 20 relocations a year. The platform's dashboard should let HR managers track the status of each relocation in real time. WhatsApp as a communication channel is essential — expats coordinate via WhatsApp even before they arrive.
Expand this. Consider: a pre-arrival digital welcome pack customised by nationality and employer type; a Nairobi orientation guide (neighbourhoods, safety, transport, culture) built into the platform; a community layer where recently arrived expats connect; a departure service (end-of-lease management, shipping coordination); an annual re-subscription for "concierge on demand" — you've been here a year and need a new mechanic, a good dentist, a holiday villa on the coast; and partnerships with global relocation companies (Cartus, Crown World Mobility) who need a Nairobi ground partner.
Hosting: Next.js on Vercel. Backend and CRM-lite on Railway. The relocation dashboard is a complex stateful app — PostgreSQL with a well-designed schema for tracking multi-step relocations. Docker-ready. Consider a lightweight CMS (Sanity or Contentful) for the curated neighbourhood and school guide content.
